Destination Travel Guides

Explore our comprehensive guides to China's most fascinating destinations, written by our expert travel team.

Southwest Culture

Yunnan Province

Discover diverse cultures, stunning landscapes, and ancient tea traditions. From UNESCO World Heritage old towns to terraced rice fields and tropical rainforests.

  • Best time: March-May, September-November
  • Must-see: Lijiang Old Town, Tiger Leaping Gorge
  • Local specialty: Pu'er tea, Yunnan ham
Read Full Guide
Plateau Spiritual

Tibetan Plateau

Explore the roof of the world with spiritual sites and natural wonders. Home to highest peaks, sacred monasteries, and pristine lakes.

  • Best time: May-October (warmer months)
  • Must-see: Potala Palace, Namtso Lake
  • Important: Altitude acclimatization required
Read Full Guide
Northwest History

Xinjiang Region

Journey along the ancient Silk Road through diverse landscapes and cultures. From bustling bazaars to stunning lakes and vast deserts.

  • Best time: May-October
  • Must-see: Kashgar Old City, Kanas Lake
  • Local specialty: Xinjiang lamb kebabs, naan bread
Read Full Guide
Southwest Ethnic

Guizhou Province

Experience rich ethnic cultures and karst landscapes. Home to 17 ethnic minority groups with authentic cultural experiences and dramatic scenery.

  • Best time: March-November
  • Must-see: Huangguoshu Waterfall, Xijiang Miao Village
  • Local specialty: Sour fish soup, rice wine
Read Full Guide
Southwest Wildlife

Sichuan Province

From pandas to spicy cuisine, discover the many faces of Sichuan. Famous for fiery cuisine, giant pandas, and stunning natural scenery.

  • Best time: March-June, September-November
  • Must-see: Chengdu Panda Base, Jiuzhaigou
  • Local specialty: Hot pot, mapo tofu
Read Full Guide
Urban History

Beijing & Shanghai

Navigate China's iconic cities. Beijing offers imperial history and traditional culture, while Shanghai showcases modern China's skyline and arts scene.

  • Best time: April-May, September-October
  • Must-see: Forbidden City, The Bund
  • Local specialty: Peking duck, xiaolongbao
Read Full Guide

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our travel services and journey planning process.

We recommend booking at least 2-3 months in advance for custom journeys to ensure availability and allow time for personalized planning. For small group journeys, booking 1-2 months ahead is usually sufficient. Peak seasons (spring and autumn) may require earlier booking.
Our journey prices typically include accommodation, transportation, professional guides, entrance fees to attractions, most meals, and 24/7 support. International flights, personal expenses, travel insurance, and optional activities are usually not included. Each journey proposal includes a detailed breakdown of inclusions and exclusions.
Yes! We specialize in creating safe and enriching experiences for solo travelers. We can design custom solo journeys or help you join small group tours. All our guides are trained to ensure solo travelers feel comfortable and included throughout the journey.
Cancellation policies vary by journey type and timing. Generally, cancellations made 30+ days before departure receive a full refund minus processing fees. Cancellations 15-30 days before receive 50% refund. Cancellations within 15 days are non-refundable. We recommend purchasing travel insurance. Full terms are provided in your booking confirmation.
Sustainability is core to our operations. We partner with eco-friendly accommodations, support local communities, minimize carbon footprints through our "Dual Carbon Plan," donate 10% of fees from western routes to ecological funds, respect cultural heritage sites, and work with UNESCO and conservation organizations. We also educate travelers on responsible tourism practices.
Absolutely! We accommodate all dietary restrictions including vegetarian, vegan, halal, kosher, and food allergies. We also work with travelers who have mobility needs, medical conditions, or other special requirements. Please inform us during the booking process so we can make appropriate arrangements.

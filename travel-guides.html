Destination Guides

Explore our comprehensive guides to China's most fascinating destinations, written by our expert travel team.

Southwest Culture

Yunnan Province

Discover the diverse cultures, stunning landscapes, and ancient tea traditions of Yunnan. From the UNESCO World Heritage old towns of Lijiang and Dali to the terraced rice fields of Yuanyang and the tropical rainforests of Xishuangbanna, Yunnan offers an incredible diversity of experiences.

  • Best time: March-May, September-November
  • Must-see: Lijiang Old Town, Tiger Leaping Gorge
  • Local specialty: Pu'er tea, Yunnan ham

Plateau Spiritual

Tibetan Plateau

Explore the roof of the world with our guide to Tibet's spiritual sites and natural wonders. Home to some of the world's highest peaks, most sacred monasteries, and most pristine lakes, Tibet offers a journey that is both physically challenging and spiritually rewarding.

  • Best time: May-October (warmer months)
  • Must-see: Potala Palace, Namtso Lake
  • Important: Altitude acclimatization required

Northwest History

Xinjiang Region

Journey along the ancient Silk Road through Xinjiang's diverse landscapes and cultures. From the bustling bazaars of Kashgar to the stunning beauty of Kanas Lake and the vast Taklamakan Desert, Xinjiang showcases the incredible diversity of China's western frontier.

  • Best time: May-October
  • Must-see: Kashgar Old City, Kanas Lake
  • Local specialty: Xinjiang lamb kebabs, naan bread
Read Full Guide
Southwest Ethnic

Guizhou Province

Experience the rich ethnic cultures and karst landscapes of Guizhou. Home to 17 ethnic minority groups, Guizhou offers authentic cultural experiences, from traditional Miao silverwork to Dong polyphonic singing, set against a backdrop of dramatic karst mountains and terraced fields.

  • Best time: March-November
  • Must-see: Huangguoshu Waterfall, Xijiang Miao Village
  • Local specialty: Sour fish soup, rice wine
Read Full Guide
Southwest Wildlife

Sichuan Province

From pandas to spicy cuisine, discover the many faces of Sichuan. This province is famous for its fiery cuisine, adorable giant pandas, stunning natural scenery including Jiuzhaigou Valley, and rich cultural heritage including ancient Buddhist sites and traditional teahouse culture.

  • Best time: March-June, September-November
  • Must-see: Chengdu Panda Base, Jiuzhaigou
  • Local specialty: Hot pot, mapo tofu
Read Full Guide
Urban History

Beijing & Shanghai

Navigate China's iconic cities with our comprehensive urban guides. Beijing offers imperial history and traditional culture, while Shanghai showcases modern China with its futuristic skyline, world-class dining, and vibrant arts scene. Both cities provide perfect gateways to understanding China's past and future.

  • Best time: April-May, September-October
  • Must-see: Forbidden City, The Bund
  • Local specialty: Peking duck, xiaolongbao
